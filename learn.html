Interactive Soldering

Resistor Soldering
Heat the pad and lead for 2-3 seconds, then apply solder. The solder should flow around the lead and pad, forming a small cone shape.
Capacitor Soldering
Ensure correct polarity (longer lead is positive). Heat the pad and lead, then apply solder. Avoid excessive heat on electrolytic capacitors.
LED Soldering
LEDs are polarity-sensitive. The longer lead (anode) connects to positive. The flat side of the LED body indicates the cathode (negative).
Header Soldering
Insert the header pins and solder one pin first. Check alignment before soldering the remaining pins. Ensure all pins are straight.

Example ProtocolCard

I²C

Inter-Integrated Circuit protocol for connecting low-speed peripherals to processors and microcontrollers.

  • Standard mode (100 kHz)
  • Fast mode (400 kHz)
  • Fast mode plus (1 MHz)
  • 7-bit and 10-bit addressing

SPI

Serial Peripheral Interface for high-speed, full-duplex communication between devices over short distances.

  • Up to 50 MHz clock rate
  • All CPOL/CPHA modes
  • Multiple chip select support
  • 3-wire and 4-wire configurations

UART/USART

Universal Asynchronous/Synchronous Receiver-Transmitter for serial communication between devices.

  • Baud rates from 110 to 12M baud
  • 5-9 data bits
  • Parity options
  • 1, 1.5, or 2 stop bits

CAN

Controller Area Network protocol designed for robust communication in automotive and industrial environments.

  • CAN 2.0A (Standard) and 2.0B (Extended)
  • Bit rates up to 1 Mbps
  • Error detection and fault confinement
  • Differential signaling analysis

1-Wire

Dallas Semiconductor protocol for communication with low-speed devices over a single data line plus ground.

  • Standard speed (15.4 kbps)
  • Overdrive speed (125 kbps)
  • Device discovery and enumeration
  • ROM commands and function commands

I²S

Inter-IC Sound protocol for connecting digital audio devices, typically used for audio data transfer.

  • Standard I²S format
  • Left-justified and right-justified formats
  • PCM/DSP mode
  • TDM (Time Division Multiplexed) mode

Device Hero

Clip:bit

An innovative micro:bit expansion board designed for rapid prototyping and educational exploration without soldering.

Overview

The Clip:bit is our flagship device that revolutionizes how students and makers interact with the micro:bit platform. By eliminating the need for soldering, it makes physical computing more accessible to beginners while providing the flexibility that advanced users need for rapid prototyping.

Key Features

  • Solderless Connections - Spring-loaded clips provide secure connections to components without soldering
  • Breadboard Compatible - Integrates seamlessly with standard breadboards for expanded prototyping
  • Full GPIO Access - Exposes all micro:bit GPIO pins for maximum flexibility
  • Built-in Protections - Voltage regulation and current limiting to protect both the micro:bit and connected components
  • Expandable Design - Compatible with a growing ecosystem of clip-on modules and sensors

Technical Specifications

  • Compatible with all micro:bit versions (V1 and V2)
  • Operating voltage: 3.3V (regulated from USB or battery input)
  • 20 spring-loaded connection points
  • Dimensions: 80mm x 60mm x 15mm
  • Weight: 45g (without micro:bit)
